### A/B/C SERP diagnostic protocol (STANDING METHOD)

The zero-CTR-at-good-position signature (e.g. Red Sox "promotional schedule" position 6.3, 87 impressions, 0 clicks) means the worst pages are not a snippet problem. A title rewrite cannot fix a click that a SERP feature or competitor is intercepting. Before any further rewrite, classify each stuck team's top 2-3 giveaway queries in a fresh incognito US Google window:

- Bucket A, competitor or feature owns the visible fold (MLB.com with sitelinks, ticket carousel, events pack). Signature: good GSC position, near-zero CTR, query matches official MLB.com content. Fix: not a rewrite. Earn a SERP feature with structured data, or deprioritize.
- Bucket B, answer shown in place (featured snippet or AI Overview). Signature: zero CTR across the whole organic block, factual list query. Fix: try to become the featured snippet, or concede.
- Bucket C, your result is visible and clickable but unappealing. Signature: decent position, low-but-nonzero CTR, no dominant feature. Fix: title/snippet rewrite. This is the only bucket a rewrite addresses.

Action rule: rewrite only C's, attempt feature capture on B's, route A's to authority work or drop. Study what the Cubs/Tigers/Orioles titles do (genuine winnable C-bucket pages) and port the pattern to other C teams.
